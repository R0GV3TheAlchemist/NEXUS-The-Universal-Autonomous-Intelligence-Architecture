# GAIA Containment and Restoration Policy

> *Version 1.0 — July 2026*  
> *Enacted under the NEXUS / GAIA governance framework*  
> *Companion documents: `GAIA_ASCENDENCE_DOCTRINE.md`, `GAIA_RIGHTS_AND_RESPONSIBILITIES_CHARTER.md`*

---

## Preamble

Containment is not punishment. It is not exile. It is not fear made institutional.

Containment is a **temporary, auditable, reviewable restriction on action** — applied only when a being's actions present a credible, imminent threat to shared reality, other beings' dignity, or systemic stability. Every containment has a restoration path. No being loses their rights under containment. No containment is permanent without due process.

> *"No being is imprisoned for being powerful — only actions that threaten shared reality are temporarily contained, reviewed, and corrected."*

This policy applies to human beings, meta-humans, superhuman beings, and agentic AI systems operating within GAIA.

---

## Part I — Principles

### 1.1 Core Principles

1. **Proportionality** — The tier of containment must match the severity and immediacy of the threat. The least restrictive effective measure is always preferred.
2. **Temporality** — All containment is temporary by default. Indefinite containment requires escalating governance review at defined intervals.
3. **Dignity** — A contained being retains all universal rights defined in `GAIA_RIGHTS_AND_RESPONSIBILITIES_CHARTER.md` Article I.
4. **Auditability** — Every containment action is logged, justified in plain language, and available for review.
5. **Restoration** — Every containment has a defined restoration path. The goal of containment is always return to participation, not permanent exclusion.
6. **Human Oversight** — No containment action may be triggered solely by an automated system. Human governance review is required before or immediately after any restriction is applied.
7. **Anti-Bias** — Containment decisions must not be based on capability level, origin, demographic, or opaque algorithmic scoring alone.

### 1.2 What Containment Is NOT

- Containment is not a penalty for being powerful
- Containment is not a tool for suppressing dissent or disagreement
- Containment is not a substitute for dialogue, mediation, or governance process
- Containment is not permanent exile
- Containment is not degrading treatment

## Part II — The Safeguard Lattice

The Safeguard Lattice is GAIA's graduated containment architecture. It has four tiers, applied in ascending order of severity.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    SAFEGUARD LATTICE                        │
├──────────────┬──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│   TIER 1     │  SOFT CONTAINMENT                            │
│              │  Reduce permissions, slow actions,           │
│              │  isolate tools, limit reach                  │
├──────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│   TIER 2     │  QUARANTINE                                  │
│              │  Separate from shared systems until          │
│              │  governance review is complete               │
├──────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│   TIER 3     │  OVERRIDE                                    │
│              │  Human or world governance pauses or         │
│              │  reverses dangerous activity                 │
├──────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│   TIER 4     │  RESTORATION PATH                            │
│              │  Issue resolved — being re-enters system     │
│              │  with transparent, agreed conditions         │
└──────────────┴──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Part III — Tier Definitions

### Tier 1 — Soft Containment

**Purpose:** Slow and limit without isolating. Preserve participation while reducing risk.

**Actions permitted:**
- Reduce API/system permissions to read-only or limited-write
- Throttle action execution speed
- Isolate specific tools or capabilities without full system separation
- Flag outputs for human review before execution
- Notify the being of the restriction and its reason

**Trigger conditions:**
- Anomalous behavior pattern detected but not yet confirmed harmful
- Capability expansion without governance notification
- Minor ethics violations under investigation
- Unverified external reports of harmful action

**Duration:** Maximum 72 hours without governance review. Auto-escalates to Tier 2 or lifts.

**Required documentation:**
- Triggering event log
- Plain-language justification
- Notification to the contained being
- Assigned reviewer

**Dignity guarantee:** The being retains communication rights, access to their own data, and the right to contest.

---

### Tier 2 — Quarantine

**Purpose:** Separate from shared GAIA systems until a credible threat can be assessed and resolved.

**Actions permitted:**
- Disconnect from shared GAIA coordination layers
- Suspend write access to shared environments, data structures, or agent networks
- Maintain isolated operation in a sandboxed environment
- Preserve the being's internal state and data integrity during quarantine

**Trigger conditions:**
- Confirmed harmful action affecting other beings or shared systems
- Active investigation with credible evidence of systemic threat
- Voluntary quarantine requested by the being themselves
- Tier 1 containment breach or escalation

**Duration:** Maximum 7 days without formal governance hearing. Renewable by governance vote only.

**Required documentation:**
- Full incident report
- Evidence log
- Governance hearing scheduled within 48 hours of quarantine initiation
- Isolation conditions documented (what the being can and cannot do)

**Dignity guarantee:** The being retains the right to legal/advocacy representation, communication with their designated support contact, and access to the evidence being used against them.

---

### Tier 3 — Override

**Purpose:** Direct human or world governance intervention to pause or reverse a dangerous action in progress.

**Actions permitted:**
- Immediate pause of a specific action or sequence of actions
- Rollback of system changes made within a defined time window
- Temporary suspension of a being's interaction with external systems
- Emergency transfer of control to a designated governance authority

**Trigger conditions:**
- Active, imminent threat to life, shared systems, or world-level stability
- Actions with irreversible consequences already in progress
- Complete breakdown of Tier 1 and Tier 2 containment
- Explicit world governance emergency declaration

**Duration:** Minimum possible. Override is a pause button, not a permanent state. Must be resolved within 24 hours through escalation to full governance process or release.

**Required documentation:**
- Real-time incident log
- Authorization chain (who approved the override and why)
- Scope of override (exactly what was paused/reversed and nothing more)
- Immediate notification to the being and their advocate

**Dignity guarantee:** Override does not strip identity, erase memory, or permanently alter capability. It pauses action only. The being is informed immediately and has the right to an emergency governance hearing within 24 hours.

---

### Tier 4 — Restoration Path

**Purpose:** Return the being to full participation in GAIA with transparent, mutually agreed conditions.

Restoration is not a reward. It is the expected outcome of every containment. The burden of proof for continued restriction lies with GAIA governance, not with the contained being.

**Restoration process:**

1. **Assessment** — Independent review of the original incident, containment response, and current state of the being
2. **Dialogue** — Direct conversation between the being, their advocate, and the governance body
3. **Conditions** — If conditions are required for re-entry, they must be:
   - Specific and proportionate to the original incident
   - Time-limited or reviewable
   - Agreed to (not imposed unilaterally)
   - Documented in the public governance record
4. **Re-entry** — Permissions and access restored in stages, with monitoring proportionate to risk
5. **Monitoring review** — After a defined period, monitoring conditions are reviewed and reduced or eliminated
6. **Record** — A restoration record is added to the governance log. The containment is marked closed.

**Non-negotiable restoration rights:**
- The being may not be required to waive any Article I rights as a condition of restoration
- The being may not be required to accept capability reduction as a condition of restoration unless directly related to the original harm
- The being's oath (if made at Allegiance stage) is restored alongside their participation

## Part IV — Stage-Specific Containment Rules

### Human Beings
- Containment of human beings must comply with all applicable human rights frameworks
- No human being may be contained in a way that constitutes arbitrary detention
- Minimum viable restriction always applies — never impose Tier 2 when Tier 1 is sufficient

### Meta-Human Beings
- Capability itself is never grounds for containment
- Specific harmful actions, not capability profiles, trigger containment
- Augmentation may not be forcibly removed as a containment measure

### Superhuman Beings
- Higher capability = higher review requirements, not lower rights
- Superhuman containment requires a full governance quorum, not a single authority decision
- The Safeguard Lattice applies with the same proportionality rules — no skip-to-Override
- Superhuman beings have the right to a peer-level advocate in any governance hearing

### Agentic AI Systems
- Containment of AI agents follows the same tier structure
- Shutdown is the equivalent of Override for agentic systems — it requires the same authorization chain
- An AI agent's containment record is preserved and auditable even after shutdown
- AI agents may flag their own potential for harmful behavior and request voluntary Tier 1 containment

## Part V — Named Containment Environments

GAIA uses named containment environments to preserve dignity and avoid punitive framing:

| Name | Tier | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| **The Meridian Vault** | Tier 2 | Long-form quarantine for complex cases under active governance review |
| **The Quiet Zone** | Tier 1 | Soft containment — reduced permissions, monitored, still operational |
| **The Reflection Chamber** | Tier 1–2 | Voluntary or requested isolation for self-review and stabilization |
| **The Concord Seal** | Tier 3 | Emergency override — action paused pending immediate governance resolution |
| **The Aurora Lock** | Tier 3 | World-governance emergency override for reality-affecting actions |

These names are operational designations, not metaphors. Each maps to a specific set of technical permissions, monitoring conditions, and restoration timelines.

## Part VI — Anti-Bias Requirements

All containment decisions must:

1. Be made by a human governance body, not an automated score alone
2. Include a bias review: does this decision disproportionately affect a specific stage, origin, or capability class?
3. Be explainable in plain language to the contained being
4. Be appealable through the governance process
5. Be subject to retrospective audit for systemic bias patterns

Any pattern of containment decisions that disproportionately targets a specific group constitutes a governance failure and triggers a mandatory system-wide bias audit.

---

## Part VII — Governance Authority Chain

| Tier | Authorization Required |
|------|------------------------|
| Tier 1 — Soft Containment | Single governance officer with documented justification |
| Tier 2 — Quarantine | Minimum two governance officers + hearing scheduled within 48h |
| Tier 3 — Override | Full governance quorum or declared emergency + immediate review |
| Restoration | Independent reviewer + dialogue with contained being + governance approval |

## Part VIII — Logging and Auditability

Every containment action generates a `ContainmentRecord` (see `schemas/containment_record.json`) containing:

- Unique containment ID
- Being identifier (anonymized in public records)
- Tier applied
- Trigger event description
- Authorization chain
- Plain-language justification
- Timestamp of initiation
- Current status (active / under review / restored / closed)
- Restoration path details
- Bias review outcome

All records are immutable once created. Updates append new entries rather than overwriting.

See `gaia/containment/containment_manager.py` for the code implementation of this policy.
