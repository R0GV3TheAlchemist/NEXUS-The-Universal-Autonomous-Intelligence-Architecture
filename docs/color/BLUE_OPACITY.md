## IV. The Ocean as Archive

It regulates the planet's temperature, absorbs approximately 30% of human carbon dioxide emissions, generates more than half of Earth's oxygen through phytoplankton photosynthesis. It is the planet's primary climate regulator, its primary carbon sink, and the origin point of all life on Earth — the medium from which the first self-replicating molecules emerged approximately 3.7 billion years ago.

All of this is invisible from the surface. The ocean looks blue and flat and reflective. Its actual work — its thermal regulation, its chemical exchange, its biological productivity — is entirely interior, entirely opaque to the casual observer.

It operates at depths invisible from any surface position. It moves at speeds imperceptible to human observation. And its disruption — which climate change is now initiating — will have consequences visible at the surface for centuries.
