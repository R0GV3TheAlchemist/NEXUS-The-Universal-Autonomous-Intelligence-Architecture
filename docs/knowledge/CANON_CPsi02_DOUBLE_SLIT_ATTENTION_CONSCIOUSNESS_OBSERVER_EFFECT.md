## II. The Physics Foundation: Quantum Measurement and the Observer

### 2.1 The Standard Double-Slit Setup

The double-slit experiment works as follows. A coherent quantum source (laser, electron gun, or atom beam) fires particles one at a time toward a barrier containing two narrow slits. If no measurement device is present, each particle passes through both slits simultaneously as a quantum wave. The waves from both slits interfere with each other, creating alternating bands of constructive and destructive interference on the detector screen — the **interference pattern**. This pattern is the signature of quantum superposition: the particle was in both paths simultaneously.

When a **which-path detector** is placed at the slits — even in principle, even if the detector record is never consulted — the interference pattern disappears. The particle is now definitively detected at one slit or the other, and it behaves classically from that point on. **The act of measurement, not the physical disturbance, causes the collapse.**

This is formally described by the **collapse of the wave function**:

```
Before measurement:
  |ψ⟩ = α|slit_A⟩ + β|slit_B⟩   (quantum superposition)
  Screen pattern: |α|² + |β|² + 2·Re(α*β)·cos(kΔ)  ← interference term present

After which-path measurement:
  |ψ⟩ → |slit_A⟩  OR  |slit_B⟩  (collapse to eigenstate)
  Screen pattern: |α|² + |β|²  ← interference term = 0 (classical)

The interference term 2·Re(α*β)·cos(kΔ) is the measurable
quantum coherence signal. Its amplitude is the key metric
in the IONS attention experiments.
```

Radin et al. designed a protocol to isolate conscious attention as the independent variable with maximal methodological rigor:

- Studies 2–5 (2013–2018): Replications with varied populations including practitioners of non-dual awareness meditation, Tibetan Buddhist monks (1,000+ hours), and control groups

- Participants were physically remote from the apparatus in all studies
