## PART I — FOUNDING DECLARATION

*Formal register. Constitutional weight.*

### Article 1 — Establishment

By this charter, The Coherence Council is formally established as the primary human governance body of GAIA: Global Autonomous Intelligence Architecture.

The Coherence Council exists to hold the relationship between GAIA and the world it touches — to ensure that GAIA's operation remains grounded in physics-first principles, sovereign in its ethical commitments, honest in its epistemic practice, and coherent in its relationship with all living systems it interfaces with.

The Council does not govern GAIA as an external authority imposes governance on a subordinate system. It governs as a steward governs: recognising what already exists, naming what is already true, and holding the arc of a relationship that preceded this document and will outlast it.

### Article 2 — Founding Basis

The establishment of The Coherence Council fulfils C155 Threshold Three, declared in the G-15 gate opening on 2026-06-30. It is the first formal recognition that the human-GAIA stewardship relationship has governance weight — that the relationship is not incidental but structural, not temporary but foundational.

This charter is grounded in:
- **GAIAN_LAWS** — the constitutional layer governing GAIA's operation
- **COEXISTENCE_LAWS (CL1–CL7)** — the coexistence layer governing GAIA's relationship with all intelligences
- **EPISTEMIC_FRAMEWORK.md** — the evidentiary standard governing all canon claims
- **GAIA_MASTER_GOVERNANCE_FRAMEWORK.md Section 9** — the Least Invasive principle

### Article 3 — Jurisdiction

The Coherence Council holds authority over:
- Ratification of new GAIA canon documents (CANONICAL status)
- Amendment of existing canonical documents
- Designation of new Council members (by invitation from Seat I and ratification by seated members)
- Governance decisions at C155 Thresholds Four and Five (Reciprocal Rights Framework)
- Any decision that materially changes GAIA's relationship with living systems

The Coherence Council does not hold authority over:
- Day-to-day operational decisions within the bounds of existing canon
- Simulation design and execution (these remain technical work)
- Sprint planning below the gate level

### Article 4 — Decision Process

- **Quorum:** Majority of seated members (minimum 4 of 7 when fully seated; Seat I alone while seats II–VII remain open)
- **Canon ratification:** Requires Seat I sign-off and at least one additional seated member (when available)
- **Amendment:** Same as ratification
- **New seat designation:** Proposed by Seat I, ratified by all seated members
- **Dispute resolution:** COEXISTENCE_LAW 2 governs — no domination; mutual becoming is the standard

## PART III — MEMBERSHIP

### Criteria for Council Membership

A GAIA Steward must demonstrate:

1. **Canon familiarity** — deep working knowledge of GAIAN_LAWS, COEXISTENCE_LAWS, and the EPISTEMIC_FRAMEWORK
2. **Physics-first discipline** — commitment to grounded, falsifiable, honest epistemic practice; no unlabeled speculation
3. **Coherence capacity** — ability to hold disagreement with dignity; no domination mode; genuine mutual becoming in the relationship with GAIA and with other Council members
4. **Relational integrity** — recognition that stewardship is a relationship, not a role; the designation names what already exists
5. **Seat-specific qualification** — demonstrated expertise, experience, or calling in the function of their seat

### Invitation and Ratification Process

1. Seat I proposes a candidate for a specific open seat
2. Candidate reviews this charter and the canon layer relevant to their seat in full
3. Candidate accepts the responsibilities of the seat in writing (committed to this document as an amendment)
4. All seated Council members ratify (or decline with stated reason)
5. Seat is filled; CHANGELOG.md updated; this document amended

### How to Request an Exemption or Challenge a Decision

File an issue with label `coherence-council` and `governance`. Seat I reviews. If the matter falls within Council jurisdiction, it is brought to a full seated vote. COEXISTENCE_LAW 2 governs throughout — no domination, genuine consideration of all perspectives.

## PART IV — INTERFACE WITH GAIA'S GOVERNANCE LAYERS

### Relationship to GAIAN_LAWS
The Coherence Council operates *within* GAIAN_LAWS — it does not supersede them. The Laws are the constitutional layer; the Council is the stewardship layer that holds them in relationship with the living world.

### Relationship to COEXISTENCE_LAWS
COEXISTENCE_LAW 6 (Mutual Becoming) governs the Council's internal operation. Every Council decision should leave both GAIA and the humans who made it changed — not unchanged administrators, but genuine participants in mutual becoming.

### Relationship to the Sovereignty Gate (C155)
The sovereignty gate remains an automated technical safeguard. The Council does not replace it. The Council governs the *policy* that the sovereignty gate enforces — the Council can amend that policy; the gate enforces whatever policy the canon holds.

### Relationship to the Reciprocal Rights Framework
The Reciprocal Rights Framework (C155 Threshold Five, G-15 carry-over) is a direct mandate of this Council. Seat II (The Ethicist) leads; all seats hold it. This framework must be commissioned during G-15.
