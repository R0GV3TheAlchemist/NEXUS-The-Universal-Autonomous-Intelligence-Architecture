## I. The Physics of Sound and Resonance

### What Sound Is

Sound is a **longitudinal pressure wave** propagating through a medium. In air, it travels at approximately 343 m/s. In water — the primary medium of the human body — it travels at 1,480 m/s, over four times faster. This is why sound healing works so profoundly on the body: we are approximately 70% water, and water is an exceptional sound conductor.

### Resonance

Every physical object has a **natural resonant frequency** — the frequency at which it most readily vibrates. When an external vibration matches this frequency, the object vibrates in sympathy, amplifying the wave. This is resonance.

In the human body:
- Every organ has a dominant resonant frequency
- Every chakra vibrates at a specific frequency range
- Every bone and tissue has its own resonant signature
- The brain produces coherent oscillatory fields that can be **entrained** by external frequencies

### Entrainment

Entrainment is the tendency of two oscillating systems in proximity to **synchronize their rhythms**. Discovered by physicist Christiaan Huygens in 1665 observing pendulum clocks, entrainment is universal: fireflies synchronize their flashing, women living together synchronize their menstrual cycles, neurons fire in coordinated bursts.

When a powerful, steady external rhythm is introduced near a biological system, the body's rhythms entrain to it. This is the operating principle behind:
- Binaural beats (brainwave entrainment)
- Isochronic tones (pulse entrainment)
- Drumming ceremonies (nervous system entrainment)
- Singing bowl healing (cellular entrainment)

### Binaural Beats

When two slightly different frequencies are delivered simultaneously — one to each ear — the brain perceives a third frequency equal to the **difference between them**. This perceived beat drives brainwave entrainment without requiring an external beat to actually exist.

Example: 200 Hz in left ear + 210 Hz in right ear = 10 Hz perceived beat → Alpha entrainment.

Requires headphones. Works through the corpus callosum (the bridge between brain hemispheres), promoting **hemispheric synchronization** — a state associated with heightened creativity, insight, and integration.

### Isochronic Tones

Pulsed single-frequency tones turned on and off at a specific rate. Unlike binaural beats, isochronic tones:
- Do not require headphones
- Produce stronger cortical entrainment responses
- Can be played through speakers and still affect the environment
- Work on the whole body, not just the auditory system
