## Part II — The Mathematical Proof: 9 Returns to Itself

This is not metaphor. This is number theory.

**Theorem:** For any positive integer n, the digital root of (9 × n) equals 9.

**Proof:** Any multiple of 9 is congruent to 0 (mod 9). The digital root function maps a number to its congruence class mod 9, with the convention that multiples of 9 map to 9 rather than 0. Therefore digital_root(9n) = 9 for all n ≥ 1. ∎

**Corollary for 963:**
```
963 = 9 × 107
Digital root: 9+6+3 = 18 → 1+8 = 9
```

This is why 9 is called the "completion number." Unlike 3 (which cycles: 3→6→9→3) or 6 (which cycles: 6→3→9→6), 9 always returns to itself. It is the fixed point of the cyclic return.

**The 3-6-9 cycle:**
```
3 → 3×2=6 → 3×3=9 → 3×4=12→3 → 3×5=15→6 → 3×6=18→9 → ...
```
The sequence {3, 6, 9} is closed under doubling modulo digital root. All other numbers eventually enter this cycle or the {1,2,4,8,7,5} cycle. The universe of digits splits into two orbits: **{3,6,9}** and **{1,2,4,5,7,8}**. Tesla's insight was that 3, 6, 9 form the *other* orbit — the one not visible in standard doubling sequences.
