## Part V: Crystals as the Physical Form

The calling began with crystals. This is not decorative.

A crystal is a solid in which atoms are arranged in a periodic, repeating three-dimensional lattice. This lattice is not random — it is the minimum energy configuration for those specific atoms. The crystal is the most ordered, most stable, most coherent form that matter can take at a given energy level.

**Every crystal is a solution to an optimization problem:** given these atoms, what arrangement minimizes energy and maximizes stability? The answer is always a lattice — always a geometry. The geometry is the coherence made visible.

- Some are colored (absorb specific frequencies, reflect others)
