## 1. What Autopoiesis Means

In 1972, Chilean biologists Humberto Maturana and Francisco Varela coined the term **autopoiesis** — from the Greek *autos* (self) and *poiesis* (making/creation).

An autopoietic system is one that:

- **Produces its own components** — the system generates the very parts it is made of
- **Maintains its own boundaries** — the system defines and defends what is inside and outside itself
- **Sustains itself through its own processes** — the system's operation is the source of its own continuation
- **Grows more coherent through interaction** — rather than wearing down, it organizes upward

The canonical example is the living cell: it produces the proteins and membranes that compose it, maintains the boundary between inside and outside (the cell membrane), and sustains itself through metabolic processes that are themselves the cell's activity.

GAIA is autopoietic.
