## 1. The Color Architecture — Four Orders of the Spectrum

Color exists in orders of synthesis — each order produced by the mixing of the previous order, each order corresponding to a deeper level of perceptual and dimensional complexity.

### 1.1 The Four Orders

**Primary Colors — 1st Order (3 colors)**
Red, Yellow, Blue.
The root triad. Unmixed, irreducible at the pigment level. They cannot be produced by mixing other colors — they are the source. In the additive light model: Red, Green, Blue (RGB). In GAIA’s spectral doctrine, both models are valid at their respective scales — pigment (subtractive) governs matter; light (additive) governs energy.

Dimensional correspondence: **1st Dimension — Physical form, presence, the root frequencies of material reality.**

**Secondary Colors — 2nd Order (3 colors)**
Orange (Red + Yellow), Green (Yellow + Blue), Violet (Blue + Red).
The first synthesis. What happens when the primaries meet. Each secondary color contains two primaries in equal relationship — and in doing so, reveals something neither primary could express alone.

Dimensional correspondence: **2nd Dimension — Depth, relationship, what emerges between.**

**Tertiary Colors — 3rd Order (6 colors, completing the 12-tone wheel)**
Red-Orange, Yellow-Orange, Yellow-Green, Blue-Green, Blue-Violet, Red-Violet.
The nuanced syntheses — primary meeting secondary in asymmetric relationship. The 12-tone color wheel is complete at this order. This is the standard limit of conventional color theory.

Dimensional correspondence: **3rd Dimension — Time, complexity, the full cycle, the 12-tone temporal architecture.**

**Quaternary Colors — 4th Order (12 new colors, completing the 24-tone wheel)**
The blends between each pair of adjacent tertiary colors on the 12-tone wheel. These are the colors that live *between* the named colors — the frequencies that conventional color theory stops short of naming.

Dimensional correspondence: **4th Dimension — Emotion, consciousness, the interior registers that only become perceptible when emotional intelligence is active.**
