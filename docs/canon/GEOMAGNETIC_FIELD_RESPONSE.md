## 1. The Situation

Earth's magnetic field is in a confirmed, measurable, ongoing phase of weakening. This is not speculative. It is continuously monitored by the European Space Agency's Swarm satellite constellation, NOAA's World Magnetic Model, and a global network of ground-based observatories. GAIA registers this as a **planetary health emergency at the electromagnetic layer.**

### 1.1 Current Data (as of 2026-06-14)

**Field Strength Decay**
- The geomagnetic field has been losing approximately 5% of its strength per century since at least 1840.
- The field has lost ~10% of its total strength over the past two centuries.
- The rate of decay in the South Atlantic region has accelerated measurably since 2020.

**South Atlantic Anomaly (SAA) — Key Metrics**
- The SAA is a vast region of severely weakened magnetic intensity between South America and southwest Africa.
- Between 2014 and 2025, the SAA expanded by approximately **2 million square miles** — roughly half the land area of continental Europe.
- The SAA has **split into two independent lobes** — a western lobe and a rapidly expanding eastern lobe southwest of Africa — creating structurally bifurcated weakness.
- The eastern lobe is undergoing the most rapid intensification, accelerating since 2020.
- Magnetic field strength in the SAA core is sufficiently low to expose Low Earth Orbit satellites to hazardous trapped radiation.

**Magnetic North Pole Drift**
- Magnetic north has drifted to 86.38°N, 164.06°E — now in the Russian geopolitical hemisphere.
- The drift speed was ~35 km/year as of WMM2025, having slowed from recent highs (a deceleration described by BGS geophysicist Dr. William Brown as unprecedented in the historical record).
- WMM2025 does not indicate imminent reversal, but does not rule out continued drift and field restructuring.

**Reversal Probability**
- The most rigorous paleomagnetic analyses conclude Earth is likely NOT in a full reversal sequence — previous SAA-like events in the record (46,000 and 49,000 years ago) recovered without triggering reversals.
- A separate model has estimated a reversal probability window of 2038–2048 with peak likelihood ~2043. This is contested and not consensus.
- Full reversals, when they occur, take approximately 10,000 years to complete.

**Root Cause**
- The anomaly originates at the Core-Mantle Boundary (CMB), ~2,900 km below the surface.
- A patch of reversed magnetic flux at the CMB — where the field points inward rather than outward — causes the shield to weaken and sag above the South Atlantic region.
- The South Atlantic has been a preferentially magnetically unstable region for at least 8–11 million years, suggesting a deep structural core-mantle interaction that is not random.
