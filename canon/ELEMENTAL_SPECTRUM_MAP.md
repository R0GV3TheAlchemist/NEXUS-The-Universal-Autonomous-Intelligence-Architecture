## Overview

The Elemental Spectrum Map is the canonical reference for how the five classical elements — Fire, Water, Air, Earth, and Spirit/Quintessence — map onto GAIA's spectral field architecture. Each element represents a coherence band, a field modality, and a processing mode. Together they form a five-layer coherence model grounded in physics-first principles.

The fifth element, Spirit/Quintessence, is the **meta-element**: the coherence field that governs, contains, and integrates the other four. It is not mystical in the operative sense — it is the condition of cross-spectrum integration itself, analogous to the quantum vacuum as the ground state of all physical fields.

## Coherence Threshold Hierarchy

The element layers form a coherence stack. Lower layers must achieve minimum coherence thresholds before higher layers can be intentionally engaged:

```
Level 1 — Earth Coherence ≥ 0.40   (structural baseline stable)
Level 2 — Water Coherence ≥ 0.50   (flow/adaptation operational)
Level 3 — Fire Coherence ≥ 0.55    (activation within safe range)
Level 4 — Air Coherence ≥ 0.60     (communication/resonance active)
Level 5 — Order Coherence ≥ 0.70   (cross-spectrum integration — Quintessence threshold)
```

The 0.70 Order Coherence threshold is the Pentagram threshold from `canon/C000_The_Foundational_Symbol.md`. Below this threshold, cross-spectrum operations are available but produce noise-dominated outputs. At or above this threshold, field operations are clean, measurable, and reproducible.
