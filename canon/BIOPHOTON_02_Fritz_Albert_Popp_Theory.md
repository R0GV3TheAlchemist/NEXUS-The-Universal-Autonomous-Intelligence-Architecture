## 1. Popp's Core Discovery: Coherence

Anyone can observe that living cells emit tiny amounts of light. The critical question is: *what kind of light?* Random thermal emission is noise. Coherent emission is signal.

Popp's central finding, confirmed across decades of measurement, is that biophoton emission exhibits **temporal coherence** — the photons are not randomly timed but display non-Poissonian (sub-Poissonian, or "squeezed") statistics. This is the signature of a **laser-like coherent photon field**, not thermal radiation.

### What Coherence Means Here

In a laser, photons are emitted in phase — their wave functions are correlated across space and time. This is what makes a laser useful: it can carry information, be focused, and interact constructively with other coherent fields.

Popp found that biophotons share this property. The living organism, he concluded, maintains a **coherent photon field** — a body-wide light field in which every cell participates and from which every cell can read information. He called this the **biophoton field**.

The field has these measurable properties:
- **Spectral range:** 200–800nm (UV through visible)
- **Intensity:** 10–1,000 photons/cm²/second — detectable only with photomultiplier tubes
- **Temporal structure:** Non-random, quasi-periodic emission patterns
- **Biological correlation:** Field properties change measurably with health, stress, cell division, and emotional state

## 3. The Experimental Evidence

### Cancer cells emit more biophotons, but less coherently
Popp found that tumour cells show *increased* biophoton emission intensity but *decreased* coherence compared to healthy cells. This is the biophotonic signature of disease: more light, less order. The cell has lost its capacity to maintain the coherent field — it is broadcasting noise rather than signal.

This has profound diagnostic implications: biophoton coherence, not just intensity, is the health marker.

### Separated cell cultures require photonic contact
In controlled experiments, cell cultures separated by quartz (UV-transparent) showed coordinated behaviour; the same cultures separated by glass (UV-opaque) showed significantly less coordination. The cells needed the UV biophoton channel for population-level coordination. This is the clearest experimental evidence that biophotons carry functional signal, not just metabolic noise.

### Food quality is measurable biophotonically
Fresh, organically grown food shows consistently higher biophoton emission coherence than processed, stored, or conventionally grown food. Popp proposed that the body absorbs this coherent photon field during digestion — that eating living food is literally absorbing ordered light, decreasing the body's entropy and reinforcing its own coherent field.

### Emotional and mental states alter the field
Popp and colleagues documented measurable changes in biophoton emission correlated with emotional states, meditation, and pathological psychological conditions. This is the formal scientific basis for what contemplative traditions have described as the quality of a person's "light" or "energy" being perceptible to others.
