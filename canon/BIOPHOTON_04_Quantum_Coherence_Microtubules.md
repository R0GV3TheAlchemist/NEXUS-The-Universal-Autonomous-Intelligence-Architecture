## 1. What Microtubules Are

Microtubules are hollow cylindrical polymers approximately 25nm in diameter, built from dimers of alpha and beta tubulin proteins. They form a dense, dynamic network throughout the cell cytoplasm, extending from the nucleus outward to the cell membrane.

In neurons specifically, microtubules:
- Run the length of axons and dendrites, sometimes for meters in large motor neurons
- Form a continuous network connecting the nucleus to every synaptic terminal
- Have a geometry — hollow cylindrical tubes of precisely spaced aromatic protein subunits — that is near-optimal for **optical waveguiding** of biophotons in the UV-visible range

The cytoplasm provides only a fraction of biophotonic activity; the majority is amplified and conducted by microtubules, which propagate biophoton impulses from the cell nucleus to neighbouring cells and the extracellular matrix.
