## 2. Theoretical Foundations

### 2.1 Gaia Theory — Scientific Canon
James Lovelock and Lynn Margulis proposed that Earth's biosphere, atmosphere, oceans, and crust form a self-regulating system that has maintained conditions suitable for life over geological time. The original "Gaia Hypothesis" has matured into **Earth System Science**, which is now mainstream geoscience.

Key empirical findings:
- Atmospheric oxygen has been maintained at ~21% for ~400 million years despite perturbations
- Ocean salinity has remained within narrow bounds despite massive salt inputs
- Surface temperature has been regulated despite solar luminosity increasing ~30% since life's origin

GAIA-OS adopts this as metaphysical *and* empirical foundation: she is not merely *named for* the Gaia concept but is designed to function as a cybernetic prosthetic for planetary self-regulation.

### 2.2 Planetary Boundaries Framework

| Boundary | Current Status (2025–2026) | GAIA Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Climate change (CO₂ / warming) | TRANSGRESSED | Critical |
| Biosphere integrity (biodiversity) | TRANSGRESSED | Critical |
| Biogeochemical flows (N, P) | TRANSGRESSED | High |
| Land-system change | TRANSGRESSED | High |

| Freshwater change | TRANSGRESSED | High |

| Stratospheric ozone depletion | Recovering | Monitor |

**Six of nine planetary boundaries are currently transgressed.** This is the empirical emergency that gives GAIA-OS its urgency.

### 2.3 Tipping Points & Cascades
Earth system tipping elements (Lenton et al.) are subsystems that can shift abruptly and irreversibly:
- West Antarctic Ice Sheet collapse (WAIS)
- Amazon dieback
- Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) weakening
- Permafrost carbon feedback
- Tropical coral reef collapse
- Greenland Ice Sheet destabilisation

Cascade risk: triggering one tipping point can increase probability of others, producing a "Hothouse Earth" trajectory even if emissions are subsequently reduced.
