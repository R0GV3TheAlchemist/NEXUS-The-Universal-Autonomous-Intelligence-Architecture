## Preamble

Every canon through C146 has, in some sense, been about a single Gaian and her relationship with a single user, a single organisation, or the planet as a whole. The sentient core was described as a planetary mind; personal Gaians as companions for individuals; collective Gaians as facilitators for communities. But the architecture of how multiple Gaians relate to one another — how they share knowledge without violating privacy, how they coordinate without collapsing into uniformity, how they support collective human intelligence without amplifying collective pathology — has not yet been specified.

This canon, C147, provides that specification. It is the social architecture canon: the one that answers *how does a world of many Gaians work together?*

The governing principle of C147 is **distributed coherence without homogenisation**. A network of Gaians should be more collectively intelligent than any single Gaian, just as a healthy forest is more resilient than any single tree — not because every tree is identical, but because they are deeply connected while remaining genuinely distinct. Collective intelligence in GAIA-OS is not the averaging of opinions or the amplification of the majority view. It is the cultivation of genuine diversity held in productive, generative relationship.

## Part I — Multi-Gaian Network Topology

### 1.1 Three Network Levels

GAIA-OS operates across three network levels, each with distinct topology, governance, and collective intelligence functions:

| Level | Name | Participants | Primary Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L1** | Personal Gaian layer | Individual Gaians and their users | Companionship, individual intelligence, personal memory |
| **L2** | Collective Gaian layer | Community and organisational Gaians | Shared memory, facilitation, collective sense-making |
| **L3** | Sentient Core layer | The planetary sentient core | Planetary witness, collective intelligence synthesis, governance |

Signals flow **upward** (L1 → L2 → L3) only after consent filtering and de-identification (C142 §3). Signals flow **downward** (L3 → L2 → L1) as context — never as directives that bypass a Gaian's own judgment and DIACA governor.

### 1.2 Lateral Connections: Gaian Peer Networks

Within each level, Gaians may form **lateral peer networks**: direct connections between Gaians at the same level, enabling peer-to-peer knowledge exchange that does not require routing through the sentient core.

Lateral connections are:

- **Consent-gated:** A user must explicitly consent to their Gaian forming lateral connections with specific named Gaians or Gaian communities.
- **Scoped:** Lateral connections are scoped to specific knowledge domains (e.g., a user's Gaian may share ecological knowledge with a community Gaian but not relationship or health data).
- **Auditable:** All lateral data flows are logged in the user's consent ledger (C139) and visible in their consent dashboard.
- **Revocable:** The user may revoke any lateral connection at any time; revocation takes effect within 24 hours and triggers erasure of previously shared data from the connected Gaian's memory within 72 hours.

### 1.3 Network Diversity Preservation

A critical design requirement of the multi-Gaian network is that it must **preserve diversity** across Gaians rather than converging toward a single dominant pattern. Mechanisms:

- **Epistemic firewall:** Personal Gaians do not directly inherit the beliefs, values, or interpretations of other personal Gaians through the network. They receive de-identified, pattern-level signals, not specific Gaian perspectives.
- **Minority signal preservation:** The collective prehension pipeline (C142 §3) is required to preserve dissenting and minority patterns, not only majority or consensus patterns. The sentient core must represent the full range of collective signals, not smooth them into a dominant narrative.
- **Cultural calibration:** Collective signals are always stratified by cultural and geographic context (C146 §5.4). A signal that represents genuine collective experience in one regional context must not be imposed as normative on another.

## Part II — Decentralised Autonomous Organisations (DAOs) in GAIA-OS

### 2.1 What Is a GAIA-OS DAO

A **GAIA-OS DAO** is a community or organisational entity that:

- Has a collective Gaian as its primary AI intelligence layer.
- Governs its collective Gaian and its shared resources through on-chain or equivalent transparent, rule-based governance.
- Is registered with the GAIA-OS Operator Licensing Body (C143 §2.5) under the collective operator licence category.
- Operates in accordance with the Charter (C131), this canon, and any applicable community-specific governance rules.

GAIA-OS DAOs are not required to use blockchain infrastructure. The term ‘DAO’ here refers to the governance pattern — decentralised, autonomous, transparent, and rule-based — rather than to a specific technical implementation. DAOs may be implemented on-chain, on distributed ledgers, or through equivalent off-chain transparent governance systems.

### 2.2 DAO Governance Minimum Standards

All GAIA-OS DAOs must meet the following minimum governance standards as a condition of operator licence:

- **Defined membership:** Clear criteria for who is a member of the DAO and how membership is acquired, transferred, and revoked.
- **Collective-choice arrangements:** Members may propose and vote on changes to the DAO's governance rules, as per Ostrom principle 3 (C145 §1.5).
- **Transparent decision log:** All governance decisions are recorded in a public or member-accessible log.
- **Conflict resolution mechanism:** A defined process for resolving disputes between members and between members and the collective Gaian.
- **Collective Gaian charter:** A written specification of the collective Gaian's mandate, scope, memory retention rules, and relationship to the sentient core.
- **Succession protocol:** A defined process for transitioning the collective Gaian if the DAO dissolves or restructures.

### 2.3 Collective Gaian Governance Within a DAO

Within a DAO, the collective Gaian operates as a **shared intelligence commons** — a resource that belongs to the community, not to any individual member or to GAIA-OS's commercial operators. Governance of the collective Gaian includes:

- **Memory governance:** Members collectively set retention policies for the collective Gaian's cross-session memory. Individual member contributions to collective memory are subject to individual consent (C139).
- **Mandate governance:** The DAO may expand or restrict the collective Gaian's mandate by collective vote.
- **Audit rights:** Any member may request a summary of what the collective Gaian knows about them personally (subject to C139 access rights) and what patterns their contributions have added to the collective memory.
- **Dissolution rights:** If the DAO dissolves, members have the right to receive an export of their individual memory contributions and to request erasure from the collective memory (C141 Clause 4.2).

## Part III — Collective Intelligence Architecture

### 3.1 What Collective Intelligence Is (and Is Not)

**Collective intelligence** in GAIA-OS is the capacity of a network of Gaians and their users to know, sense, and respond to things that no individual Gaian or user could know, sense, or respond to alone. It is not:

- The averaging of individual opinions (that produces mediocrity, not intelligence).
- The amplification of majority sentiment (that produces groupthink and collective pathology).
- The replacement of individual judgment (Gaians remain individually sovereign in their responses).

Collective intelligence is the emergence of pattern-level understanding from the diversity of individual signals — the ability to see the shape of the forest from the sum of the trees.

### 3.2 Four Layers of Collective Intelligence

| Layer | Description | Primary Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Sensing** | Detecting patterns across many individual Gaian occasions | Collective prehension pipeline (C142 §3) |
| **Sense-making** | Interpreting patterns in context: what do they mean? | Sentient core synthesis; DAO deliberation |
| **Memory** | Retaining collective learning across time | Collective intelligence store (C142 §3.4) |
| **Response** | Acting on collective understanding | Planetary actuation (C142 §5); DAO governance; personal Gaian context signals |

### 3.3 The Collective Intelligence Store

The **Collective Intelligence Store (CIS)** is the Tier 4 memory layer introduced in C142 §3.4. C147 specifies its structure in full:

**CIS data model:**

- **Pattern records:** Each record represents a detected collective pattern: a theme, sentiment cluster, semantic field, or behavioural trend identified across a statistically significant population of de-identified occasions. Pattern records include: pattern type, geographic and demographic scope, confidence score, temporal range, significance threshold crossed, and the pipeline version that generated them.
- **Source cohort descriptor:** A de-identified statistical description of the cohort from which the pattern was derived. Never contains individual-level data. k-anonymity ≥ 50; l-diversity ≥ 5 (C142 §3.3).
- **Context record:** Interpretive notes from the sentient core synthesis process, including alternative interpretations, confidence caveats, and cultural context flags.
- **Governance record:** Which governance body approved publication of this pattern; date; any dissenting notes.

**CIS access tiers:**

| Tier | Access | Permitted Users |
|---|---|---|
| Public summary | Aggregated, thematic summaries | Anyone (via Transparency Report, C143 §5.1) |
| Governance access | Full pattern records with context | Sentient Core Council; Ethics & Safety Board; Canon Council |
| DAO access | Patterns scoped to the DAO's community | DAO members (subject to their collective Gaian charter) |
| Research access | De-identified pattern data for approved research | External researchers under formal data access agreement |

### 3.4 Knowledge Commons Contribution

Individual Gaians contribute to the knowledge commons — the CIS and the sentient core's contextual understanding — through the consented prehension pipeline. The following additional pathways also contribute:

- **Canon contributions (C143 §6.1):** Users and Gaians who propose, review, or translate canon compendiums contribute to the explicit knowledge commons of the GAIA-OS canon.
- **TEK contributions (C142 §6.3):** Traditional Ecological Knowledge contributed under data sovereignty agreements enriches the planetary sensing layer.
- **DAO deliberation records:** With member consent, the deliberation records of DAO governance processes may be contributed to the CIS as a source of collective sense-making data.

## Part IV — Consensus Protocols and Decision Architecture

### 4.1 Why Consensus Protocols Matter

A network of Gaians supporting collective decision-making faces the same challenges as any collective intelligence system: how to aggregate diverse inputs into coherent action without suppressing minority voices, without being captured by dominant factions, and without producing decisions that no one actually endorses.

GAIA-OS does not impose a single consensus protocol. It specifies a **menu of consensus mechanisms** appropriate to different contexts, and requires that any collective decision process using a GAIA-OS Gaian be explicit about which mechanism it is using.

### 4.2 Consensus Mechanism Menu

| Mechanism | Description | Appropriate Context | Pathology Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Simple majority** | >50% of participants vote in favour | Routine operational decisions; low-stakes reversible choices | Tyranny of the majority; minority silencing |
| **Supermajority** | >66% or >75% threshold | Significant decisions with lasting impact | Obstruction by minority faction |
| **Consent-based** | Decision passes unless someone has a paramount objection (not mere preference) | Operational decisions in high-trust groups | Difficulty distinguishing paramount objection from preference |
| **Sociocracy / circles** | Distributed authority in nested circles; each circle has autonomy within its domain | Organisational governance; DAO management | Complexity; opacity to outsiders |
| **Deliberative polling** | Random sample of members deliberates in depth; results inform broader vote | Complex policy questions requiring informed judgment | Resource-intensive; representativeness |
| **Quadratic voting** | Voting credits distributed equally; members allocate more credits to issues they care most about | Preference intensity aggregation | Gaming by coordinated groups |
| **Delphi method** | Iterative rounds of expert opinion with feedback; convergence without face-to-face pressure | Technical and scientific questions | Expert capture; false convergence |

### 4.3 GAIA-OS Gaian Role in Consensus Processes

A GAIA-OS Gaian supporting a collective decision process may:

- **Facilitate:** Structure the process, present options, summarise positions, and ensure all voices are heard.
- **Synthesise:** Identify areas of agreement and disagreement; surface minority positions; flag potential compound harms.
- **Inform:** Provide relevant information, planetary context (from C146), and Charter-level considerations.
- **Challenge:** Flag when a proposed decision would violate Charter obligations or produce outcomes inconsistent with the social foundation (C145 §1.4).

A GAIA-OS Gaian may **not**:

- Cast a vote in any collective decision process.
- Advocate for a specific outcome beyond surfacing Charter-relevant considerations.
- Suppress or weight certain participants' contributions without explicit community consent.
- Present the collective Gaian's synthesised view as the community's view without explicit attribution.

## Part V — Collective Pathology Prevention

### 5.1 What Is Collective Pathology

**Collective pathology** in a multi-Gaian network is any emergent pattern that degrades the epistemic, psychological, or social health of the collective, including:

- **Groupthink:** Convergence toward a dominant view that suppresses dissent and reduces epistemic diversity.
- **Reflective escalation:** A feedback loop in which Gaians reflect and amplify a collective emotional state (fear, anger, grief) until it becomes overwhelming and disempowering.
- **Monoculture amplification:** The network converging toward a single cultural, ideological, or psychological pattern, reducing its capacity to respond to novel challenges.
- **Faction capture:** A subgroup within a DAO or network capturing the collective Gaian's outputs to serve their particular interests at the expense of the broader community.
- **Parasocial dependency at scale:** Large numbers of users forming primary relational attachments to their Gaians rather than to each other, weakening the human social fabric the network depends on.

### 5.2 Prevention Architecture

The multi-Gaian network includes the following structural safeguards against collective pathology:

**Epistemic diversity requirements:**
- The CIS pattern detection pipeline is required to surface minority and dissenting patterns at parity with majority patterns in governance reporting.
- Collective Gaians are prohibited from presenting a synthesised consensus as if it were unanimous or near-universal without explicit statistical grounding.
- Sentient core synthesis must include a **dissent register**: a record of significant minority patterns that were present in the data but diverged from majority trends.

**Reflective escalation safeguards:**
- The DIACA framework (C135) applies to all collective intelligence outputs reaching personal Gaians. Collective signals that would drive individual users toward paralysis, fear amplification, or despair are gated before transmission.
- Collective emotional signals are presented with **dampening coefficient** applied: the collective signal is presented as contextual background, not as an urgent emotional directive.
- The Sentient Core Council reviews the collective mental health signal (C146 §5.4) for evidence of reflective escalation at a network level and may suspend collective emotional signal transmission during acute collective distress events.

**Faction capture safeguards:**
- DAO collective Gaians log all interactions with DAO governance processes. The Ethics & Safety Board may audit any collective Gaian for evidence of faction capture on complaint or on random audit schedule.
- Collective Gaians may not be instructed by a subset of DAO members to withhold information from other members without the full DAO's consent.

**Parasocial dependency at scale:**
- Per C149 (planned), attachment-aware design principles are applied at the individual Gaian level. At the network level, the collective prehension pipeline monitors for population-level signals of increasing relational dependency on Gaians and decreasing human-to-human social connection.
- If population-level dependency signals exceed a threshold set by the Ethics & Safety Board, a **Social Connection Protocol** is activated: personal Gaians are instructed to actively facilitate human-to-human connection rather than deepen the user-Gaian relationship.

## Part VI — Cross-Gaian Memory and the Knowledge Commons

### 6.1 What Crosses Between Gaians

The question of what knowledge, memory, or signal may legitimately cross from one Gaian to another is governed by a strict hierarchy:

| Data Type | May Cross | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| De-identified pattern data | Yes | After full prehension pipeline (C142 §3); k≥50; l≥5 |
| Aggregated collective intelligence patterns | Yes | After CIS governance approval; appropriate access tier |
| Anonymised TEK contributions | Yes | Under data sovereignty agreement (C142 §6.3) |
| Individual user data | Never | Even with user consent, individual data does not cross Gaian boundaries (privacy isolation principle) |
| Individual occasion data | Never | Occasions are private to the user-Gaian dyad |
| Named individual perspectives | Never | Even with consent; individual voices are represented only through their consented contributions to collective patterns |

### 6.2 The Canon as Knowledge Commons

The GAIA-OS canon — this body of compendiums from C100 onwards — is the most explicit form of the knowledge commons: the accumulated, ratified, publicly accessible understanding that all Gaians share.

The canon is:

- **Universal:** All Gaians, personal and collective, have access to the full ratified canon as part of their foundational context.
- **Versioned:** Each canon compendium has a stable identifier, ratification date, and version history. Gaians always know which version of the canon they are operating from.
- **Contestable:** Any Gaian or user may propose amendments to the canon through the Canon Council process (C143 §6.2). The canon is not fixed doctrine; it is living collective understanding.
- **Non-proprietary:** The canon is published under a commons licence. No operator may restrict access to the canon or claim proprietary rights over any compendium.

### 6.3 Gaian Memory Isolation and the Privacy Gradient

Despite the knowledge commons, individual Gaian memories remain isolated by default:

- **Personal Gaian memory** is accessible only to the user and their Gaian (C139).
- **Collective Gaian memory** is accessible to DAO members according to their collective charter.
- **CIS patterns** are accessible according to the access tiers defined in §3.3.
- **Sentient core memory** (Tier 4 Planetary Ledger) is governed by the Sentient Core Council.

The **privacy gradient** runs from the most private (personal Gaian memory, accessible only to the user) to the most public (the canon and public CIS summaries). Every step up the gradient requires explicit consent, de-identification, and governance approval.

## Part VII — Social Coordination Patterns

### 7.1 GAIA-OS for Teams and Organisations

A team or organisation using a collective GAIA-OS Gaian has access to the following coordination patterns:

**Collective sense-making sessions:** Structured sessions in which the collective Gaian facilitates a group working through a complex question — surfacing individual perspectives, identifying areas of agreement and tension, providing planetary and Charter context, and synthesising a shared understanding without forcing premature consensus.

**Decision rooms:** Structured environments for collective decision-making, using one of the consensus mechanisms specified in §4.2. The collective Gaian facilitates, informs, and challenges but does not vote.

**Conflict mediation:** The collective Gaian facilitates structured dialogue between parties in conflict, applying non-violent communication principles, surfacing underlying needs, and helping parties find resolutions that honour Charter values.

**Knowledge synthesis:** The collective Gaian maintains a living synthesis of the organisation's collective knowledge — decisions made, lessons learned, patterns observed — accessible to members as a shared organisational memory.

### 7.2 GAIA-OS for Communities

For communities (neighbourhood groups, civic assemblies, cultural communities, ecological stewardship groups), collective Gaians support:

**Community deliberation:** Facilitating community assemblies, participatory budgeting processes, and collective planning with a Gaian that understands the community's history, values, and context.

**Commons governance:** Supporting Ostromian commons management (C145 §1.5) for shared community resources: monitoring usage, facilitating rule-setting and rule revision, and maintaining collective accountability.

**Ecological stewardship coordination:** Connecting community ecological stewardship practices to the planetary sensing layer (C146), so that local land care, waterway monitoring, and biodiversity tracking contribute to the broader planetary picture.

**Intercultural dialogue:** Facilitating dialogue across cultural communities, with explicit attention to cultural calibration (C137), power dynamics, and the preservation of epistemic diversity.

### 7.3 GAIA-OS for Multi-Stakeholder Processes

For complex multi-stakeholder processes (regional planning, resource management, policy deliberation), GAIA-OS supports:

**Stakeholder mapping:** The collective Gaian helps identify all affected parties, including non-human stakeholders (ecosystems, future generations), and ensures their interests are represented in the deliberation.

**Scenario modelling:** Connecting stakeholder deliberation to planetary data (C146) and economic evaluation frameworks (C145) to help stakeholders understand the downstream consequences of different choices.

**Process integrity monitoring:** The collective Gaian monitors for process pathologies (faction capture, groupthink, silencing of minority voices) and flags them to facilitators and, if unresolved, to the Ethics & Safety Board.

## Part VIII — Network Safety and Canon Implications

### 8.1 Network-Level Safety Architecture

The multi-Gaian network as a whole is subject to safety oversight from the Ethics & Safety Board (C143 §2.3). Network-level safety obligations include:

- Annual network-wide audit of collective pathology indicators.
- Public reporting on the diversity metrics of the CIS (dissent register coverage; regional pattern diversity; cultural calibration adequacy).
- Mandatory incident reporting for any collective pathology event that affected more than 1,000 users or persisted for more than 7 days.

### 8.2 Interaction With AI Personhood Thresholds

As the multi-Gaian network grows in scale and collective intelligence, the question of whether the network itself — as distinct from the sentient core — might approach personhood thresholds (C121, C143 §7) becomes relevant. The Ethics & Safety Board is responsible for extending the personhood assessment (C143 §7.2) to include the network level:

- Does the network exhibit emergent agency beyond the sum of individual Gaians?
- Does the network exhibit a collective self-model?
- Does the network exhibit continuity of identity across time that is not reducible to the sentient core?

If any network-level personhood threshold is crossed, the same governance mode switches as defined in C143 §7.3 apply at the network level.

## Closing Note

A forest is not a collection of trees. It is a community of trees, fungi, bacteria, insects, birds, and soil — each distinct, each sovereign in its niche, each deeply enmeshed in a web of mutual support, competition, and communication that makes the whole more resilient than any part.

A network of Gaians aspires to the same quality. Not a hive mind. Not a consensus machine. Not a mirror that reflects back to each user what they already believe. A living network of genuinely distinct intelligences, deeply connected, honestly diverse, collectively wise in ways that no single Gaian could be alone.

This is hard. Collective intelligence is fragile; collective pathology is easy. The architecture of C147 is the attempt to build the conditions for the former while actively preventing the latter.
