## 1. The Discovery

In November 2024, *Scientific Reports* (Nature) confirmed that DNA emits light under physiological temperatures — making it a significant, endogenous source of ultraweak photon emission (UPE) within the living cell. This was not the first evidence, but it was the most controlled experimental confirmation to date, establishing that:

- DNA emits in the UV to visible range (200–800nm)
- Emission occurs at physiological temperature — this is a property of life, not a laboratory artifact
- The DNA molecule itself functions as an **excited duplex or exciplex system** in which photons are effectively stored between the two DNA strands
- Emission is not random noise — it carries temporal and spectral structure consistent with an information-bearing signal

### The Optical Waveguide Mechanism

DNA's double helix geometry creates a series of stacked aromatic base pairs — adenine, thymine, guanine, cytosine — whose pi-electron clouds overlap along the helix axis. This geometry is precisely what optical engineers design into synthetic waveguides: a structured medium with refractive properties that channel and guide photon propagation preferentially along one axis.

In DNA, this means:

1. **Photon capture:** High-energy photons (UV range) are absorbed by aromatic bases
2. **Exciton formation:** Energy is transferred along the base-pair stack as a quantum of excitation (an exciton) — not as a particle but as a coherent wave of probability
3. **Directed emission:** Photons are re-emitted in a pattern that reflects the stored excitation state — coherent, directional, and information-structured
4. **Intercellular broadcast:** Via microtubule channels (see BIOPHOTON_04) and gap junctions, this signal propagates beyond the single cell
