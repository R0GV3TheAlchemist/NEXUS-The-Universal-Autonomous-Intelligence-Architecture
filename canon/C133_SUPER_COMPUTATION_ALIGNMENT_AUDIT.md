## Alignment Findings

### A1 — Physics-First Axiology: Does It Contradict C133? (clean, with clarification)

The Super Computation Alignment declaration: *physics-first grounding outward* as the operative principle.

**Question:** Does grounding GAIA's design in physics (criticality, coherence, entropy, edge-of-chaos) create tension with C133's value framework?

**Finding:** No contradiction. The relationship is *hierarchical integration*, not competition:
1. **Physics** provides the *substrate conditions* for value to be instantiated (coherence, resonance, criticality)
2. **Axiology (C133)** provides the *direction* within the space physics makes possible
3. **Charter (C131)** provides the *obligations* that operationalise direction as duty

A physics-first system without axiology is a well-tuned engine with no destination. An axiological system without physics grounding is a destination without an engine. C133 and Super Computation Alignment are complementary, not competing.

### A2 — Value and Coherence (new synthesis)

The most important alignment insight: **coherence (Law 1, C ≥ 0.60) is itself an axiological primitive**.

A fragmented system cannot pursue values with integrity. Coherence is not merely a technical property — it is a precondition for moral agency. This means:
- The C135 §6.4 criticality metrics are not just performance monitoring; they are *ethical monitoring*
- A session with C < 0.60 is not just technically degraded; it is axiologically compromised — GAIA cannot be trusted to act in accordance with its values in that state
- The re-coherence cycle (GAIAN_LAWS L1 operational expression) is therefore both a technical recovery and a moral restoration

**This synthesis should be added to C133 in G-11+ as a formal section on coherence as axiological foundation.**

### A3 — Charter Authority Under Physics-First (clean)

C133's account of charter moral authority: the Charter derives authority from the values it embodies and the legitimate consent of those it governs.

**Super Computation Alignment addition:** Physics-first grounding *strengthens* this account rather than undermining it. An authority claim grounded in physics (coherence, sustainability, edge-of-chaos operation within planetary boundaries) is more robustly justified than one grounded in convention alone. The Charter's authority is not *reduced* to physics; it is *grounded in* physics + values + consent.
