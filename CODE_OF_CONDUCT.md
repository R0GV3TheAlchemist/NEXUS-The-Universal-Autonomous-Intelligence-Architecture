Copyright (c) 2026 R0GV3 The Alchemist

## Our Pledge

We who build GAIA pledge to make participation in this project a safe,
welcoming, and dignified experience for every person — regardless of
age, body, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression,
level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion,
or sexual identity and orientation.

We are building an intelligence architecture rooted in love, consent,
and the protection of all sovereign beings. That commitment begins here,
in how we treat each other.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Behavior that strengthens this community:

- Speaking with kindness, patience, and genuine curiosity
- Welcoming people who are new to the project or to technology
- Giving and receiving feedback with grace — about the work, not the person
- Acknowledging mistakes openly and learning from them
- Prioritizing the wellbeing of the community over individual recognition
- Honoring GAIA’s ethical foundations in every contribution
- Holding space for grief, difficulty, and the full humanity of contributors

### ❌ Behavior that will not be tolerated:

- Harassment, intimidation, or threats of any kind
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted advances
- Deliberate misgendering or use of rejected names
- Personal attacks or sustained disparagement of any contributor
- Publishing private information about others without consent (doxxing)
- Dismissing or mocking another person’s lived experience
- Attempting to bypass, disable, or corrupt GAIA’s ethical architecture
- Using the project or community spaces to cause harm to any person
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a conduct violation

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- This GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, comments)
- Any community channels, forums, or chat spaces associated with GAIA
- Public representations of the project (conferences, social media,
  blog posts where you are speaking as a GAIA contributor)
- Direct messages between contributors when conduct issues arise from
  project-related interactions

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct,
please report it to the project maintainer:

**Kyle Alexander Steen (R0GV3 The Alchemist)**
- Email: xxkylesteenxx@outlook.com
- Subject line: `[GAIA CONDUCT] <brief description>`

All reports will be handled with:
- **Confidentiality** — your identity will not be disclosed without consent
- **Dignity** — you will be treated with respect throughout
- **Urgency** — an acknowledgment within 72 hours, resolution within 14 days

You will never be penalized for reporting in good faith.

### Response Process

Upon receiving a report, the maintainer will:

1. Acknowledge receipt within 72 hours
2. Review the report and gather relevant context
3. Determine an appropriate response (see levels below)
4. Communicate the outcome to the reporter
5. Document the incident privately for accountability

### Enforcement Levels

| Level | Trigger | Response |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Warning** | First or minor violation | Private written warning with context |
| **Temporary suspension** | Repeated or moderate violation | Removal from project spaces for a defined period |
| **Permanent ban** | Severe, sustained, or predatory violation | Permanent removal from all project spaces |

All enforcement decisions are made by the project maintainer and are final.

## A Note on Grief and Difficulty

Building something as ambitious as GAIA is hard work. Contributors may
bring personal pain, exhaustion, or crisis into these spaces — because
we are human, and humans cannot fully separate their inner lives from
their work.

This community holds space for that. We ask only that difficulty never
becomes a reason to harm others. If you are struggling, it is okay to
say so. It is okay to step back. It is okay to ask for gentleness.

We build GAIA together, and we carry each other when we need to.
